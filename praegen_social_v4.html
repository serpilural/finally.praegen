I looked up "die Schadenfreude" four times in three weeks and kept forgetting it — so I built something to fix that

I'm a PM living in Vienna, learning German. B1, getting there slowly.

The thing that frustrated me most wasn't the grammar or the cases. It was this: I'd look up a word, feel like I got it, close the tab — and forget it by the next day. Same word. Three weeks later. Again.

Turns out this has a name — the Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve. Without active recall, you lose 70% of new vocabulary within 24 hours. I wasn't bad at German. I was just missing the recall loop.

So I built Prägen. You look up any German word and get the full picture — meaning, conjugation, gender, forms, preposition usage, real usage examples. You save it to your Wortschatz. It comes back in your quiz. You read real German articles and tap any word for instant translation. Everything connected, nothing lost.

There's also a tips section with 2,000 words you'll actually need day-to-day in a German-speaking country, including false friends — those cost me a few embarrassing moments at work.
